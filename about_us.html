Mission Statement

Our Mission as MHYC (Mental Health Youth Center(s)) is to help guide our youth / young adults in the U.S to create a great and sustainable mental health by providing the youth with free therapy and great experiences. We would want to eliminate the struggles we face with their mentals health.

MHYC Story

MYHC stands for Mental Health Youth Centers. I made it real simple, but with complex meaning and strong meaning. The MHYC is a nonprofit organization, founded in San Diego by Jonathan Dooley an African American engineer who was unnerved by the dramatic juxtaposition between the mental health illness created by society's image and the high risk of decreasing emotional intellect. (To overcome this formidable difficulty/ To vanquish thy challenge) Jonathan created a nonprofit that would provide the youth and young adults with the materials, a safe space, mentors, and counselors they would need to increase their emotional intellect and improve their mental health.

About Me

Hey, My name is Jonathan Dooley Im from Compton California and i'm the founder and CEO of MHYC. I like to watch anime, listen to music, and draw (Sometimes). I am a part of the Hidden Genius Project which helped envision my goal. I am a Highschool senior who just wants to leave a good impact on the world. I created MHYC because I used to struggle with Mental health issues and did not have anyone to talk to, especially professionally. So i created MHYC for the youth and young adults to have a safe and FREE space to deal with problems they might have with their mental health

Future Plan

In envisioning the future of our mental health nonprofit, we see a vibrant and compassionate organization that serves as a beacon of hope and support for individuals in need. Our comprehensive plan includes expanding our outreach programs to underserved communities, offering accessible teletherapy services, and establishing partnerships with local schools and workplaces to promote mental wellness. We aim to create a nurturing environment where everyone feels empowered to seek help without stigma. Through fundraising efforts and community events, we will raise awareness about mental health issues and fund innovative research initiatives. Our goal is to foster a society where mental health is prioritized and every individual has the resources they need to thrive emotionally and psychologically. Together, we can build a future where mental health support is readily available and no one feels alone in their journey towards healing and recovery.
